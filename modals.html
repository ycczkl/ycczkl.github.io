NSF Homepage

A homepage redesign for NSF.gov focused on scalable UI components, accessibility, and trust.

Visit NSF.gov↗

ABOUT

A complete homepage overhaul for the National Science Foundation. It transforms a dense, legacy government platform into a modernized, intuitive ecosystem using a highly flexible, USWDS-aligned component system.

THE STORY

Designing for a massive federal platform requires balancing complex, dense content with a trustworthy, contemporary aesthetic. The original NSF homepage struggled with fragmented navigation, often burying critical funding opportunities and agency priorities below the fold. I partnered closely with PMs, engineers, and content strategists to completely modernize the information architecture, establishing a clear, visually compelling entry point for millions of users.

Instead of just a surface-level reskin, we built a forward-looking, modular architecture. I designed a suite of custom responsive components—from a dynamic, video-anchored hero section that breathes modern energy into the agency's mission, to sleek, scalable "What's New" modules—ensuring they aligned with the U.S. Web Design System (USWDS). Every interaction state and mobile layout was refined through rigorous design loops to guarantee an inclusive and seamless experience across all devices.

The most meaningful impact wasn't just the visual refresh, but the underlying system. By collaborating directly with developers to integrate these new modules back into the agency's broader design system, we bridged the gap between design intention and technical implementation—creating a sustainable, easily updatable foundation that future-proofs the NSF's digital presence.

Google News: Feature Expansion

Improving Google’s news aggregation experience through new feature design.

View on New Page↗

ABOUT

A strategic feature expansion for the Google News iOS app designed to drive user retention. By introducing an immersive "No-Ads Mode" and a unified "Personal Feed," this project seamlessly integrates new user value into a highly established Material Design ecosystem.

THE STORY

In a crowded digital media landscape, user retention heavily relies on the signal-to-noise ratio. Through deep qualitative research and sentiment analysis of app store reviews, I identified a critical retention gap in the existing news experience: users were frequently overwhelmed by intrusive advertisements and struggled to surface hyper-relevant content from their subscribed sources.

To solve this, I conceptualized two high-impact feature additions aimed at reducing cognitive load and empowering user autonomy. First, I introduced a dedicated "Personal Feed"—a streamlined, chronologically ordered hub that aggregates user-followed topics and local sources, bridging the gap between algorithmic curation and explicit user intent. Second, I designed a "No-Ads Mode" that strips away visual clutter, offering a frictionless, typography-driven reading environment for deep focus.

The true challenge of this project wasn't just designing new interfaces, but successfully weaving them into Google's mature product architecture. By strictly adhering to Material Design paradigms and leveraging the app's existing navigation structures, I ensured these additions felt like native, organic extensions rather than bolted-on afterthoughts. The result is a refined content discovery loop that thoughtfully scales an enterprise-level product without breaking the users' established mental models.

Zeit: Conceptual E-Commerce

Architecting a trustworthy digital storefront for the world’s first time-travel service.

View on New Page↗

ABOUT

A speculative end-to-end e-commerce platform designed to democratize time travel. This project explores how to apply rigorous, conversion-optimized UX patterns to an unprecedented, high-stakes product category, balancing adventurous discovery with absolute user trust.

THE STORY

How do you design a booking experience for a product that defies the laws of physics? Commercializing time travel presents extreme UX challenges: it requires managing highly abstract inventory (eras, timelines, historical events) while overcoming profound user anxieties regarding safety and reliability. The strategic goal of Zeit was to make booking a trip to the 1969 moon landing feel as secure, intuitive, and frictionless as booking a weekend Airbnb.

To achieve this, I synthesized competitive insights from both extreme tech ventures (e.g., space tourism) and traditional hospitality platforms. Rather than relying on sci-fi clichés, I architected a highly structured e-commerce taxonomy. I developed a unique search and filtering ecosystem that allows users to navigate abstract concepts—such as historical periods, paradox risks, and timeline alignment—through familiar, grounded interaction patterns.

Visually and structurally, the platform was engineered to establish immediate brand authority. By employing a premium, editorial visual language and a transparent, step-by-step checkout flow, the design systematically dismantles user hesitations. This conceptual project demonstrates the ability to take an incredibly ambiguous, futuristic value proposition and ground it into a scalable, user-centric, and conversion-driven digital reality.

Data Visualization · FinTech UX · 2026

Market Valuation & Sentiment Hub

Translating complex financial data into actionable visual narratives.

View on New Page↗

ABOUT

A client-commissioned web platform designed to track market valuations and investor sentiment. The project transforms dense quantitative data into a sleek, modular dashboard, balancing analytical rigor with an intuitive, modern user experience.

THE STORY

In the FinTech space, the primary UX challenge is cognitive overload. Financial professionals and investors are constantly bombarded with raw data, making it difficult to extract rapid, actionable insights. For this client project, the objective was to architect a web-based dashboard that tracks market "Valuation & Mood" without replicating the cluttered, intimidating interfaces typical of legacy financial terminals.

I led the end-to-end UI/UX design, anchoring the experience in a sophisticated dark-theme aesthetic. This was a strategic functional choice as much as a stylistic one: it reduces visual fatigue for users monitoring screens all day, while allowing complex data visualizations—such as multi-line historical paths, scatter plots, and sentiment gauges—to surface clearly through high-contrast color coding.

To make the data digestible, I structured the information architecture to tell a progressive story. The layout guides the user from high-level, scannable summaries (like the "Sentiment at a glance" gauge) down to granular, interactive historical charts. By establishing a scalable system of modular data-viz components, I helped the client deliver a polished, enterprise-grade tool that empowers users to effortlessly navigate and interpret market complexities.
